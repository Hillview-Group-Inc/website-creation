Website vs Facebook Page: What Local Businesses Actually Need

April 2026
8 min read

If you run a local service business (whether you’re a painter, landscaper, cleaner, roofer, or contractor) you’ve probably asked yourself:

“Do I really need a website, or is a Facebook page enough?”

It’s a common question, especially for small businesses trying to keep costs low. Social media platforms like Facebook are free, easy to use, and already familiar. But relying on a Facebook page alone can limit your growth.

In this article, we’ll break down the differences between a website and a Facebook page, explain what each one does best, and help you understand what your business actually needs to attract more customers.

The Role of a Facebook Page

A Facebook page is a powerful tool for connecting with your audience. It allows you to:

  • Post updates about your business
  • Share photos of your work
  • Communicate with customers through messages
  • Collect reviews and recommendations

For local businesses, Facebook is especially useful for staying visible in your community. Many customers browse Facebook to discover local services, check reviews, or see recent activity.

It’s also a great platform for:

  • Announcing promotions
  • Posting before-and-after photos
  • Engaging with comments and messages

However, while Facebook is helpful for engagement, it has limitations when it comes to generating consistent, high-quality leads.

The Limitations of Relying Only on Facebook

Many local businesses rely entirely on Facebook, but this approach has several drawbacks.

1. Limited Visibility on Google

When people need a service, they usually start with a Google search, not Facebook.

If someone searches:

  • “painter near me”
  • “roof repair in [city]”
  • “cleaning service nearby”

Google prioritizes websites in its results. Without a website, your business is far less likely to appear in those searches.

That means you’re missing out on customers who are actively looking for your services.

2. You Don’t Control the Platform

Your Facebook page exists on a platform you don’t own.

Facebook can:

  • Change its algorithm
  • Limit how many people see your posts
  • Restrict or suspend accounts

This means your visibility can drop overnight without warning.

A website, on the other hand is something you control. It's your digital property, not dependent on a third-party platform.

3. It Looks Less Professional

When customers compare businesses, they often view a website as a sign of professionalism.

A Facebook page alone can sometimes give the impression that a business is:

  • New
  • Small-scale
  • Less established

Even if that is not true, perception matters. A professional website builds trust immediately.

4. Limited Customization

Facebook pages follow a standard layout. You cannot fully customize how your business is presented.

With a website, you can control:

  • Design and branding
  • Layout and structure
  • Messaging and content
  • Customer journey

This allows you to guide visitors toward taking action: like calling or requesting a quote.

The Power of a Website

A website is your business’s online headquarters. It is where potential customers go to learn about your services and decide whether to contact you.

Here’s what a website does best.

1. Helps You Show Up in Google Searches

One of the biggest advantages of a website is visibility.

When your site is optimized with the right keywords (like “landscaping services in Dallas” or “house cleaning in Atlanta”), it can appear in search results when customers are looking for those services.

This is how many local businesses generate consistent leads.

2. Builds Stronger Trust

A well-designed website gives your business credibility.

It allows you to showcase:

  • Your services
  • Your experience
  • Customer testimonials
  • Photos of your work

When customers see a professional website, they are more likely to trust your business and reach out.

3. Captures Leads Automatically

Websites are designed to convert visitors into customers.

Features like:

  • Contact forms
  • Quote request forms
  • Click-to-call buttons

make it easy for customers to take action.

Unlike Facebook, where customers have to message you and wait for a reply, a website provides a clear path for immediate contact.

4. Works 24/7

Your website is always available.

Even when you are busy or offline, potential customers can:

  • Learn about your services
  • View your work
  • Submit inquiries

This constant availability helps you capture leads at any time of day.

Website vs Facebook: Which One Do You Actually Need?

The answer is not one or the other.

You need both because they serve different purposes necessary for your business to bring in more customers.

Facebook Page = Engagement Tool

Use Facebook to:

  • Stay connected with your audience
  • Share updates and photos
  • Collect reviews
  • Build community trust

Website = Lead Generation Engine

Use your website to:

  • Show up on Google
  • Convert visitors into customers
  • Present your business professionally
  • Capture leads consistently

How They Work Together

The most successful local businesses use Facebook and their website together.

For example:

  • A customer finds your business on Facebook
  • They click the link to your website
  • They review your services and portfolio
  • They submit a contact form or call you

This combination creates a seamless customer journey.

Your Facebook page drives traffic.

Your website converts that traffic into leads.

What a Simple Website Should Include

If you are just getting started, your website doesn’t need to be complicated.

A basic site should include:

  • Homepage
  • About page
  • Services page
  • Gallery or portfolio
  • Contact page

Even a simple website can significantly improve your online presence and help you attract more customers.

Final Thoughts

A Facebook page is great for visibility and engagement, but it has limitations when used alone. A website provides the structure, credibility, and functionality needed to turn visitors into paying customers.

For local service businesses, relying only on Facebook can hold you back. Adding a professional website gives you a major advantage in attracting and converting customers.

If you want to grow your business, increase leads, and compete effectively, a website is not optional, it is essential.
