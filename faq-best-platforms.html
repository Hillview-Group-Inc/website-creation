Best Platforms to Build a Small Business Website

Common Questions
5 min read

Choosing the right platform to build your small business website is one of the most important decisions you'll make for your online presence. The platform you select will determine how easy it is to manage your site, what features you can offer, and how much you'll pay over time.

For a small business, the best website platforms balance ease of use, flexibility, and cost. Popular choices include Wix, Squarespace, and WordPress.org. Wix and Squarespace offer intuitive drag-and-drop editors, beautiful templates, built-in SEO tools, and hosting bundled into one subscription, which keeps things simple for non-technical owners. WordPress.org is more flexible and scalable, with thousands of themes and plugins, but it requires separate hosting and a bit more setup. Shopify is ideal if the business is primarily e-commerce, while platforms like Webflow suit those wanting more advanced design control. When choosing, consider how often the site will be updated, who will manage it, and whether you expect advanced features later.

Popular Website Building Platforms

Wix: Best for Beginners

Wix offers an intuitive drag-and-drop editor that makes it easy for non-technical owners to create beautiful websites without coding knowledge. With hundreds of templates specifically designed for service businesses, built-in SEO tools, and hosting bundled into one subscription, Wix keeps things simple and affordable.

  • Drag-and-drop editor requires no coding
  • 500+ templates to choose from
  • Built-in SEO and marketing tools
  • Hosting included in all plans
  • App market for additional functionality

Squarespace: Best for Design

Squarespace is known for its stunning, professionally designed templates that work great for creative and service-based businesses. Like Wix, it offers an intuitive interface with hosting bundled in, making it a solid choice for business owners who want a polished look without technical complexity.

  • Award-winning template designs
  • Built-in e-commerce capabilities
  • Mobile-responsive by default
  • Integrated analytics and SEO
  • 24/7 customer support

WordPress.org: Best for Flexibility

WordPress.org is more flexible and scalable than website builders, with thousands of themes and plugins available. However, it requires separate hosting and a bit more technical setup. It's ideal if you anticipate needing advanced features later or want complete control over your website.

  • Unlimited customization options
  • Thousands of plugins for any feature
  • Full ownership of your content
  • Scales from simple blogs to complex e-commerce
  • Large developer community for support

Shopify: Best for E-commerce

Shopify is the ideal platform if your business is primarily focused on selling products online. While it's overkill for simple service businesses, it's the gold standard for e-commerce with robust inventory management, payment processing, and shipping integrations.

Webflow: Best for Design Control

Webflow suits those wanting more advanced design control without coding. It bridges the gap between visual website builders and professional development tools, offering pixel-perfect design control while generating clean code.

Key Factors to Consider When Choosing

When selecting a platform for your small business website, consider these important factors:

  • Update frequency: How often will you need to update content? Some platforms make this easier than others.
  • Who will manage it: Will you maintain the site yourself or hire someone? Consider the learning curve.
  • Future features: Do you anticipate needing advanced features like booking systems, e-commerce, or membership areas later?
  • Budget: Factor in both upfront costs and ongoing monthly/annual fees.
  • Support: Consider how much technical support you'll need as a non-technical owner.

Our Recommendation for Service Businesses

For most local service businesses: cleaners, landscapers, contractors, and handymen, we typically recommend starting with Wix or Squarespace. These platforms offer the best balance of affordability, ease of use, and professional results without requiring technical expertise.

However, if you're working with a professional web designer (like Hillview Group), WordPress often provides the best long-term value, offering more flexibility for custom features and better SEO capabilities as your business grows.
